Resources in
Portland, Oregon

The first place you'll want to sign up is the Oregon ONE account. This site is run by the Oregon government and can help you get food, medical, childcare and cash benefits.

Another option is to apply for the free or low cost Oregon Health Plan. You can also apply for this through the Oregon One account.



Food Resources

The Oregon Food Bank

Twice a month a person is eligible to visit Preston's Pantry.

The C3 Food Pantry is open Tuesday's from 12pm-1pm.

Our House of Portland is for people living with AIDS/HIV. They have many resourses including food and in-home care for those living with the disease.

At the foodbank in St.Marks Lutheran Church people eligible must live in the 97206 zipcode. Those who are eligible can receive one food box per month. They also have a program specifically for seniors above age 60, no referral is necessary.

At Sunshine Division people can request the food box be delivered.

Neighborhood House offers two food boxes per month. According to their website each box should last about 5-8 days.

Francis Center is a food bank for east county residents and it says they offer food to the homeless. They also request that visits be limited to once per month.

Sisters of the Road provides free hot lunches to people in the Portland area at their pickup window. They also have coffee and other hot beverages.

Crossroads is a food bank that offers food on the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd, Thursdays of each month from 9 am to 1 pm.

EM Oregon allows anyone to get free food, there is no specific area they serve, the food is open to all regardless of location.

The Salvation Army has a food bank in Happy Valley where they supply a food box once per week that lasts around 2-3 days.

Blanchet House offers food among other resources.

Catholic Charities of Oregon provides many services including free food.


Clothing Resources

St. Marks also offers free childrens clothing on the 4th Saturday of each month.

Sunshine Division has both used and new clothes for all.

The Francis Center provides free clothing to all people in need, men women and children.

At Todd's Corner provides those with HIV/AIDS clothing and household items.

Blanchet House offers clothes among other things.


Free Health, Wellness, and Job Finding Services

If you have already tried to get the free Oregon Health plan but have not been able to get it then Cascadia Whole Health Care is another good option. They also provide affordable housing for people with restricted income.

Catholic Charities provides many resources for people of all religions including Family Services, Refugee Services, Finaancial Wellness, Interculteral Counseling, housing and more.

Central City Concern provides housing resources, health resources, recovery resources, and job resources with access to free job training.


Transportation Resources

Biketown for All is a resource for people to take the Portland bikes for free for 60 minutes and will receive a free monthly pass of unlimited 60 minute rides. People who qualify are Oregon Trail (SNAP) cardholders, TriMet Low Income Fare paticipants, residents in affordable housing, residents who qualify for utility assistance, or those who receive other social services including students on financial aid.

Ride Connection offers older adults and people with disablities free rides to get meals, go to medical appointments, shopping, and recreation.

While not free, TriMet offers discounted bus fares for the Honored Citizen pass.


Other Resources

For more general resources that can be found throughout the United States this site provides ample information for many things including, free Food, utility bill help, free money, extra income, public assistance, disability programs, section 8 housing, rent assistance, telephone assistance, loans and more.
